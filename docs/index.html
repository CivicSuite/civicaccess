CivicSuite / CivicAccess

Public notices everyone can read — and you can stand behind.

CivicAccess helps your office catch accessibility problems before a notice goes out, trade jargon for plain language, draft other-language versions for review, and keep a clean, exportable record of every check. So when a resident — or an auditor — asks, you can show your work in seconds.

Early release · v0.4.0

What it does for your office

Paste in a draft notice and CivicAccess checks it for the things that actually trip up readers — a missing title, an image with no description, wording that’s too dense — and hands back specific fixes, each tied to the accessibility standard behind it. It rewrites stiff municipal language into words residents use, prepares draft translations for a qualified reviewer to approve, spells out what an accessible form or tagged PDF needs, and walks an ADA Title II review step by step.

Every saved review and export lands in your CivicSuite records with a time-stamped note of who did what. A public-records request or an accessibility complaint becomes a quick lookup instead of a scramble through old email.

What you can count on

  • Clear fixes, not vague warnings. Each issue comes with the exact change to make and the standard it meets.
  • Plain language. Turns “remit payment prior to the deadline” into “pay before the deadline.”
  • Translations marked as drafts. Ready for a human reviewer — never sent out as official on their own.
  • ADA Title II review support that guides the work without pretending to be certification.
  • A complete paper trail. Every review is saved and exportable, ready for the next records request.

What it won’t do

CivicAccess is a tool for your staff, not a rubber stamp. It doesn’t give legal advice, certify ADA compliance, or issue official translations, and it never publishes anything on its own — you and your ADA coordinator always make the final call.

We version honestly: this is v0.4.0, an early release we stand behind for evaluation and pilots — not a finished 1.0, and not a compliance guarantee.
